Publications


Rebelle Society

Rebelle Society is a digital publication that describes itself as “a unique, revolutionary online magazine publishing daily acts of creative rebellion and celebrating the art of being alive through words and mixed media.”

Over the years, a variety of pieces I have written have been published. Some essays are stand-alone ponderings, but others belong together and describe some of the insights I had whilst going through leg reconstruction surgery and rehabilitation.

  • It’s the presence of reality that helps you heal
  • The fixing of my legs: a disjointed mermaid
  • I will dance again
  • Memories in the starlight
  • What if we choose for life to be a piece of art?
  • Heroes and heretics: no one has the gift of foresight

Nouveau

In 2020 Tommy Wieringa - a renowned Dutch author - organised a writing competition on the topic of scars, in partnership with Nouveau magazine. My submission, which focussed on getting over my fear of bearing my surgery scars on the beach, was selected for publication in the June 2021 edition of the print magazine. My essay was also published online.

  • Een zeemeermin (Dutch, translation: A mermaid)
